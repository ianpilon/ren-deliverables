## Published Articles & Thought Leadership

### "The Promise of AGI and Neuroscience, or 'Hold your Horses'" (Feb 2023)
**Published:** Galois, Inc. blog

**Central thesis:** Critical examination of claims that large language models herald AGI, arguing that our understanding of the brain is still in its infancy and our basic assumptions may be incorrect.

**Key arguments:**

**1. Historical pattern of metaphors:**
- 17th century: Brain works like hydraulics (Descartes)
- 18th century: Brain works like electricity (Galvani)
- 19th century: Brain works like telegraph wires (Helmholtz)
- 20th century: Brain works like a computer (McCulloch & Pitts)
- **Implication:** Our current metaphor is likely wrong too

**2. Computational Theory of Mind (CTM) limitations:**
- CTM assumes mental states/processes are realized through computation
- Two approaches: symbolic (computationalist) and non-symbolic (connectionist)
- Even if brains are computational, doesn't mean LLMs are brain-like

**3. Key neuroscience challenges for AI:**

**Neurons are not "dumb" integrators:**
- Single neurons can solve XOR problems (once thought impossible)
- Much more complex representational capabilities than believed

**Synaptic changes are not just computational:**
- New synaptic processes physically grow or wither with experience
- Pre/post-synaptic ends physically enlarge with learning
- Neurogenesis: entirely new neurons created (hippocampus, piriform cortex)
- Brain changes hardware dynamically vs. neural nets changing weights

**Cellular diversity vastly exceeds models:**
- 50+ different interneuron types in V1 (visual cortex) alone
- Neurons vary morphologically and functionally
- Multiple synapse types: axo-dendritic, axo-axonic, dendro-dendritic

**Glial cells unaccounted for:**
- Outnumber neurons 3:1
- Active participation in learning and synaptic plasticity
- Totally absent from neural net models

**Whole-brain intelligence:**
- Intelligence is property of whole brain, not just neocortex
- Multiple subcortical systems support complex cognition
- AI may overemphasize cortex (Gates Notes reference)

**4. The meso-level theory problem:**
- To achieve AGI, models may need to be more brain-like and biologically-inspired
- Need better meso-level theories of how neural circuits dynamically behave
- Problem identified in 2012 during Human Connectome Project, still unresolved

**5. Multiple realizability caveat:**
- AGI might arise from architectures very different from brain
- Like automobiles replacing horses without mimicking horse physiology
- But: some researchers argue AGI requires more brain-like models

**Notable quote at opening:** "We assume that the neuron is the basic functional unit, but that might be wrong. It might be that thinking of the neuron as the basic functional unit of the brain is similar to thinking of the molecule as the basic functional unit of the car, and that is a horrendous mistake." — John Searle

**Tone:** Skeptical but constructive — not dismissing AI progress, but urging realistic assessment of gaps

## Intellectual Framework & Perspectives

### On AI and Consciousness
- **Pragmatic approach:** Defining consciousness is not a prerequisite to implementing it
- **Critical of scientism:** Questions rigid materialistic descriptions
- **Buddhist influences:** References Vajrayana philosophy of self and consciousness
- **Duality of self:** Self emerges from self/other distinction
- **Implementation-focused:** Interested in *building* consciousness, not just describing it

### On Memory Architecture
- **Episodic memory:** Event-specific experiences with temporal/spatial context
- **Semantic memory:** General knowledge and concepts
- **Integration crucial:** AI teammates need both episodic and semantic systems working together
- **Autobiographical structure:** Hierarchical organization (lifetime periods → general events → specific events)
- **Future memory:** Episodic future thinking as important as past recall

### On AGI Development
- **Skeptical of hype:** Critical of anthropomorphizing chatbots
- **Brain-inspired necessity:** May need more biological fidelity for true AGI
- **Meso-level gap:** Need theories at circuit/system level, not just neuron or whole-brain
- **Multiple realizability paradox:** AGI might not need to be brain-like, but current approaches may be insufficient

### On Neuroscience-AI Gap
- **Implementation details matter:** Hardware/software distinction breaks down in biology
- **Complexity underestimated:** AI models missing vast biological complexity
- **Dynamic architecture:** Brain continuously reorganizes vs. static neural net architectures
- **Whole-system intelligence:** Overemphasis on cortex misses subcortical contributions
